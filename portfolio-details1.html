T-shirt Ordering System


Project OverView

The Product

T-shirt ordering system is a system which will provide the users to order t-shirt based on their preference size through online. This application will provide an service in such a way that user can order the size and color of t-shirt based on their preference. This application targets customers like smartphone users who are willing to complete shopping online

The Problem

Those people who are busy working and not able to go through the different types of t-shirt in shop. and those customers who wants to pre-order t-shirt easily without visting the shop.

My Role

My role: UX designer

Project duration

October 20, 2022 to November 10, 2022

The Goal

Design an website for T-shirt order that allows users to easily order any types of t-shirt.

Responsibilities:

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping,conductiong usability studies, accoubting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.




User Research

Summary

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I'm designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through reseach was working adults who don't have time to go to shop.
This user group confirmed initial assumptions about T-shirt ordering system, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from going for shopping. Other user problems included obligations, interests, or challenges that make it difficult to go to shop in person.

Pain Point

Time

Working people are too busy to spend time on shopping.

Accessibility

Platforms for ordering t-shirt are not equipped with assistive technologies

IA

Sometimes text-heavy menus in apps are often difficult to read and made order.

Findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Finding from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fadelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 Findings

1. Users want to order food quickly

2. Users want more customization options

3. Users want a delivery option

Round 2 Finding

1. The checkout process has too many unnecessary steps

2. Got confused between delivery and pickup
