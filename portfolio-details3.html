User Research



Summary

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I'm designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through reseach was working adults who don't have time to cool meals.
This user group confirmed initial assumptions about food ordering app customers, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from cooking at home. Other user problems included obligations, interests, or challenges that make it difficult to get groceries for cooking or go restaurants in person.

Usability study



Findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Finding from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fadelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 Findings

1. Users want more customization options

2. Users want a delivery option

Round 2 Finding

1. The checkout process has too many unnecessary steps

2. Got confused between delivery and pickup
